Introduction

My name is Valló Gábor. Although I've always been interested in programming (I wrote text adventure games during university back in the 1990s), I graduated from History and English language and started working as a teacher. I spent 20 years teaching in different secondary schools and also translating books. Over the years I often thought about a carrier change - everyone with knowledge about the state of the education system knows the reasons. Finally in 2022 I got an opportunity: I went through the 4 weeks foundation course called Újratervezés 3.0. Here I could get an insight into the world of programming from the basics, and I instantly liked it. Therefore I went through Ruander's 10 months Junior Frontend developer course where I could consolidate my knowledge. I plan to pass the vocational exam this autumn.

I'm aware that I'm at a slight disadvantage in carrier change because of my age, yet I'm still optimistic, for three reasons. First, I feel I'm able to understand the logic of programming, I learn new languages and frameworks fast. Second, during teaching I developed those soft skills that are also needed in this profession, e.g. communication, working alone or in a group, patience and precision. Third, I think this is a good time for change, because a large amount of educational material is available on the net, practically anything can be learnt with commitment, and also programmers are much needed in the labor market.

My hope is to find employment as a frontend programmer / website developer. This attracts me mainly because I think here I can use my creativity as well. Of course I'm also interested in backend, I also develop these skills.
